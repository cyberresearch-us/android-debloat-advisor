# Family Link and parental controls (official resources)

Uninstalling apps with ADB **does not replace** parental controls. A child can still install new apps (if allowed), use WebView inside apps, or go online on other devices. Use **Google Family Link** (or your platform’s equivalent) together with any debloat plan.

## Official Google Family Link

- **Home / get started:** [families.google.com/familylink](https://families.google.com/familylink/)
- **Help center:** [support.google.com/families](https://support.google.com/families/)
- **Set up Family Link:** [support.google.com/families/answer/7107346](https://support.google.com/families/answer/7107346)
- **Filter mature content & websites:** search Family Link help for “filters” and your child’s device type (Android / Chromebook).

## What to configure (checklist)

1. **Child Google account** supervised under your family group.
2. **Family Link app** on **your** phone + **child’s** phone (or child signed in with supervised account).
3. **App approval:** require your approval for new installs from Play Store where possible.
4. **Web / content filters:** turn on age-appropriate filtering (options vary by region and account type).
5. **Daily limits & bedtime** if you want schedule control.
6. **“Install unknown apps”:** on the child device, review which apps are allowed to sideload APKs; disable for browsers/file apps if you use strict lockdown.

## Samsung-specific

Samsung has additional settings (e.g. Kids Mode / parental features) that change by One UI version. Use Samsung’s support site for your model + “parental controls” or “Samsung Kids.”

## This project

**Android Package Advisor** only helps you **label packages** and **generate uninstall commands**. It does **not** configure Family Link. Use the links above for setup.
